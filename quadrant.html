Project 1

Quadrant

With the world taking a digital approach to all transactions, it is a need for people of today's society to have a safe method to send and receive money to friends, family, and merchants. Wouldn't it be nice to have an application that securely allows a user to add banks/cards to a digital platform, budget their money for personal or business use, and can pay anybody or entity? That is where Quadrant comes in, a responsive mobile digital payment platform that allows users to take control of their finances on the go.

My roles in this project were UX Designer, UI Designer, User Research, and User Testing. This project took approximately 5 months to complete.

Understanding the Problem

The first step was to get a better understanding of what problems users are having and what their needs are. Users need a way to shop, travel, or send money online without a card or have to use a physical bank or store to do so because of the growth in online shopping and transactions as well as problems that users face with security and efficiency.

Problems include:
  • Users have to insert information for multiple cards on apps
  • The lack of efficiency and ease of use making transactions
  • Information from the last purchase is not saved
  • A bank account or debit card is always needed
  • Information is not being properly secured with banks and cards

Competitive Analysis

The next step was to understand how the competition was providing service to users so I conducted a competitive analysis for Google Pay and PayPal. My finding was that:

  • The major advantage that PayPal has is the fraud protection that is being used in its platform
  • The major advantage that Google Pay has is that it is supported by all 4 major banks in the U.S.
  • Paypal uses TV Commercials, social media channels, and outdoor advertisements to gain users
  • GooglePay advertises based on spending habits of users and partnerships with Mastercard, American Express, Discover, and Visa

User Research

The next step was to conduct research with users to understand how to build this platform. This gives me a chance to see what users are currently using to address the problem of shopping, sending, and receiving money digitally (if they are using any platform) and see the pros and cons they face.​​​​​​​

After interviewing 3 users, these were the results of what was important to users:
  • Security
  • Ease of user
  • Accessibility on the go
  • Using a budgeting platform
  • Can link social media
  • Least amount of ads

User Personas

The Next step was to create user personas based on the research done to this point. This allows me to put a face to the person that I am designing for and I decided to create two personas. After creating the personas it was time to create User Flows for them to complete tasks as they are using Quadrant.

  • This helped me pay attention to the details of each function
  • One was focused on sending a payment to friends & family
  • The other was focused on setting up a new budget

Information Architecture

After that it was time to create the architecture library of how Quadrant would manuever.

I decided to conduct an open card sorting test on Optimalsort so that I could possibly get new names for my categories. I gathered insights from 5 participants and they averaged at about 10 minutes per session.

Testing

I tested with 6 participants moderated and unmoderated on features within the prototype. Full testing documents can be viewed here.

View Full Testing Documents

Results:

  • The user noticed All buttons did not work on initial clicks
  • After payment was sent, users did not know whether the transaction was successful or not
  • Thought the questions were too general within the budget feature

Improvements:

  • Add confirmation screen after payment sent
  • A new set of buttons for Payment, Budget, Payment features because too hard to distinguish
  • More detail is recommended for the budgeting feature

Conclusion

Users want a financial platform that will allow them to have a digital wallet to store their banks/cards that helps them budget and monitor their spending while also allowing them to spend anywhere without their physical card. With the use of Quadrant, users will be able to do all of that plus securely send payments to friends and family from the palm of their hand.

Moving forward improvements for future use include:

  • Assurance when it comes to security within the product which I feel is important to provide users with that peace of mind
  • Budgeting feature needs an option to easily be able to change the guidelines a user sets for their budget
  • Another sign-in option such as a face or fingerprint scan so users can log in quicker
